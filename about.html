About Me

I'm a social scientist and writer who turned into a UX designer. It's been a long and exciting way driven by the passion for understanding people and their behaviour. Today, instead of books and institutions, technology has the greatest influence on our lives. I just really want to be there where the magic happens. With my passion and my knowledge. ​ With my background, it is maybe obvious that my main interest in UX lies in research and insights. I love to dig deep and find patterns and forms in something that once looked like chaos. Please, go ahead and take a look at my work.

What I Can Do

User Research

I just love asking questions. And analysing the answers. I guess I really like people. Observing them and trying to understand them.

Personas

I have a lot of experience in creating imaginary people. After all, I used to write fiction.

User Flows & Journeys

I am good at visualising thoughts and action patterns. My mentor mentioned that during my education. It sounded so nice that I decided to believe him.

Information Architecture

My father was a librarian. I used to work for him when I was young. I helped him to organise old dusty books. It is actually not that far from organising digital information, except for the dust. I don't miss the dust.

Wireframing & Prototyping

I can use tools like Sketch and inVision to create wireframes and prototypes. But I am no graphic designer. I am more interested in the content and the structure than the details in appearance. I want things to work.

Usability Analysis & Testing

Oh, I love testing, too. To see the products through the users' eyes. To walk with them through the flows.
